The one point worth naming — accepting a cosmetic delta technically means the site changed visually, which brushes against the "不调整网站视觉" non-goal — is the consciously-accepted consequence of the irrecoverable theme (user-override 100). The design now converts that from silent drift into an explicit, evidenced, owner-signed decision, which is the honest resolution, not a defect.

## Pre-mortem (mandatory)

Assume the spec is implemented exactly as written. No **directional** failure remains: the problem is right, the approach is canonical, alternatives are exhausted, scope is bounded. The only residual risk is **execution-level, not design-level**: whoever runs Phase B could misclassify a genuinely material difference as "cosmetic" and obtain a rubber-stamp sign-off, letting a real regression through under jdy's name. The spec has already minimized this the way a design can — it defines the cosmetic/material boundary by concrete effects (layout, readability, navigation, light/dark), requires per-page screenshots + stated cause, forces the decision before the Pages switch (upstream of the irreversible step), and makes material differences an unconditional block. Beyond that, the risk is human judgment during execution, which is outside design scope and does not indict the direction.
